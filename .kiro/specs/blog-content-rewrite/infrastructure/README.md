### First-Time Setup (75 minutes)

1. **Read Core Documents** (60 min):
   - [ ] QUICK-START-GUIDE.md (15 min)
   - [ ] content-guidelines.md (30 min)
   - [ ] quality-validation-checklist.md (15 min)

2. **Review Templates** (15 min):
   - [ ] templates/README.md (5 min)
   - [ ] Relevant category template (10 min)

### For Each Article (2.5-3.5 hours)

1. **Preparation** (30-45 min):
   - Use keyword-research-process.md
   - Conduct topic research
   - Review relevant template

2. **Writing** (60-90 min):
   - Follow QUICK-START-GUIDE.md workflow
   - Reference content-guidelines.md as needed
   - Use category template structure

3. **Optimization** (20-30 min):
   - Create unique excerpt
   - Add internal links
   - Optimize for featured snippets
   - Verify keyword integration

4. **Quality Review** (15-20 min):
   - Complete quality-validation-checklist.md
   - Proofread and polish
   - Final validation

## Key Principles

### Quality Standards
- **Uniqueness**: No duplicate content between articles
- **Specificity**: Topic-specific content, not generic
- **Actionability**: Provide implementable recommendations
- **Authority**: Demonstrate deep industry knowledge
- **Value**: Genuinely helpful to target audience

### SEO Standards
- **Keyword Integration**: Natural, not forced (1-2% density)
- **Unique Excerpts**: Every article has distinct excerpt
- **Internal Linking**: 2-4 relevant links per article
- **Featured Snippets**: Optimize for snippet opportunities
- **Heading Structure**: Proper H2/H3 hierarchy with keywords

### Technical Standards
- **Markdown**: Valid syntax, no HTML
- **Frontmatter**: All required fields present
- **Encoding**: UTF-8 with Unix line endings
- **Filenames**: Unchanged from original
- **Dates**: Preserved from original

## Common Questions

### Q: Which document should I read first?
**A**: Start with QUICK-START-GUIDE.md for the workflow, then read content-guidelines.md for detailed standards.

### Q: Do I need to read everything before starting?
**A**: Read QUICK-START-GUIDE.md and content-guidelines.md completely. Other documents can be referenced as needed.

### Q: How strictly should I follow the templates?
**A**: Templates provide structure and required elements. Follow the overall structure closely, but adapt content elements to fit your specific topic.

### Q: What if my article doesn't fit neatly into one category?
**A**: Use the primary category template as your base, then incorporate relevant elements from secondary categories.

### Q: Can I create my own shortcuts or processes?
**A**: Yes, as long as you meet all quality standards and requirements. The documents provide guidelines, not rigid rules.

### Q: How do I know if my article is good enough?
**A**: Complete the quality-validation-checklist.md. If all items are checked, your article meets standards.
