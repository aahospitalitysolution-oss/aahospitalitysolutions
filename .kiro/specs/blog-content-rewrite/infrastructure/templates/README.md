## How to Use These Templates

### Step 1: Identify Article Category
Look at the article's category in the frontmatter to determine which template to use.

### Step 2: Review Template Structure
Read through the relevant template to understand:
- Required sections and headings
- Content elements to include
- Keywords to integrate
- Examples and benchmarks

### Step 3: Conduct Keyword Research
Use the `keyword-research-process.md` guide to identify:
- Primary keyword
- Secondary keywords (3-5)
- Long-tail variations (3-5)

### Step 4: Research Topic-Specific Content
Gather:
- Industry statistics and benchmarks
- Technology platforms or tools (if applicable)
- Case studies or examples
- Current trends and developments

### Step 5: Write Using Template Structure
Follow the template's:
- Introduction structure
- Main body sections (H2/H3 hierarchy)
- Content element requirements
- Conclusion format

### Step 6: Validate Against Checklist
Use the template's checklist to ensure:
- All required elements included
- Category-specific requirements met
- Keywords properly integrated
- Quality standards achieved

## Common Elements Across All Templates

### Universal Requirements:
1. **Introduction (150-250 words)**:
   - Compelling hook
   - Context and relevance
   - Preview of content

2. **Main Body (800-1000 words)**:
   - 4-6 H2 sections
   - 6-12 H3 subsections
   - Specific examples and data
   - Actionable recommendations

3. **Conclusion (100-150 words)**:
   - 3-5 key takeaways
   - Forward-looking statement
   - Call-to-action

### Universal Quality Standards:
- Minimum 1200 words
- Topic-specific headings
- No template language
- Unique excerpt
- Primary keyword integration
- 2-4 internal links
- Professional tone
- Actionable insights

## Template Customization Guidelines

### When to Adapt Templates:

1. **Hybrid Topics**: Some articles span multiple categories
   - Use primary category template as base
   - Incorporate elements from secondary category
   - Example: "Security and Privacy" (Technology + Operations)

2. **Unique Angles**: Some articles require special approaches
   - Follow template structure
   - Adjust content elements to fit topic
   - Maintain quality standards

3. **Regional Focus**: Articles with Southeast Asia emphasis
   - Use standard template
   - Add regional data and examples
   - Include local market considerations

### What NOT to Change:
- Overall structure (intro, body, conclusion)
- Word count minimums
- Heading hierarchy (H2/H3)
- Quality requirements
- SEO optimization standards

## Support and Questions

### If You Need Help:
1. Review the content guidelines document
2. Check the quality validation checklist
3. Look at completed article examples
4. Refer to the design document for requirements

### Common Questions:

**Q: What if an article doesn't fit neatly into one category?**
A: Use the primary category template as your base, then incorporate relevant elements from the secondary category.

**Q: Can I deviate from the template structure?**
A: The overall structure (intro, body, conclusion) and quality standards must be maintained, but you can adapt content elements to fit the specific topic.

**Q: How strictly should I follow the word counts?**
A: Minimum word counts are requirements. Target ranges are guidelines. Focus on providing comprehensive value rather than hitting exact numbers.

**Q: What if I can't find specific data or benchmarks?**
A: Use industry estimates, cite sources when available, and focus on providing the most accurate information possible. Note any data limitations.
